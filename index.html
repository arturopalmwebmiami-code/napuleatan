Own Your Ordering System

NapulEATan currently sends customers to order.yourmenu.com to place orders. This is one of the most damaging technical decisions for long-term Google visibility — and it also costs real money every month.

Current Setup — YourMenu (Third Party)
  • Google sees nothing

    Dish names, ingredients, Italian terms, and prices all live on yourmenu.com — not on napuleatan.com. Google indexes the third-party domain, not yours. Zero SEO benefit from your menu content.

  • Keyword surface area = 0

    "Margherita with San Marzano tomatoes," "wood-fired buffalo mozzarella" — all of that rich content is invisible to Google searches. You're paying with your own differentiation for someone else's domain.

  • Customers leave your site

    Every click to YourMenu is a user leaving napuleatan.com. Google measures dwell time and bounce rate. Sending users off-domain on arrival signals low engagement — a negative ranking factor.

  • Monthly fees with no equity

    You pay for the platform every month and build zero brand authority in return. When you stop paying, the ordering page is gone. Nothing accumulated.

  • No customer data ownership

    Email addresses, order history, and repeat customer behavior lives in YourMenu's database — not yours. You cannot run email campaigns, loyalty programs, or personalized offers to your own customers.

  • Branding is theirs, not yours

    Customers ordering on yourmenu.com see YourMenu's interface, not your brand. Brand recall suffers — customers remember the ordering platform, not the restaurant.

Recommended — On-Domain Ordering
  • Every menu item becomes a keyword

    When your menu lives at napuleatan.com/menu, Google crawls every dish name, ingredient, and description. "Fresh buffalo mozzarella fort lauderdale," "San Marzano pizza," "wood-fired neapolitan" — all indexed, all searchable.

  • Menu schema unlocks rich snippets

    An on-domain menu allows you to add Menu + MenuItem JSON-LD schema. Google can display your dishes, prices, and descriptions directly in search results — before a customer even clicks your site.

  • Users stay on your domain

    Longer sessions, lower bounce rate, more internal page views — all positive engagement signals that Google uses to validate local ranking decisions.

  • You own all customer data

    Email addresses, order frequency, average order value, favorite dishes — all in your database. Build a loyalty program, run birthday promotions, reactivate lapsed customers. That data is an asset that compounds.

  • One-time build, permanent equity

    An on-domain ordering page built once keeps working indefinitely. No monthly platform fees. The SEO benefit accumulates every month — it doesn't reset when you stop paying.

  • Full brand experience

    Customers order inside your branded interface — your colors, your photos, your story. Brand recall increases. Repeat orders go to your site, not a platform that also lists your competitors.

How the Filtering Works

1. QR Code Scan

Customers scan a QR code on their table or receipt before leaving. No app download needed — opens directly in mobile browser.

2. 4–5 Stars Route to Google

Happy customers are immediately prompted with a direct link to the Google Business Profile review form. This dramatically increases positive review velocity — the #1 local pack ranking factor.

3. 1–3 Stars Are Intercepted

Unsatisfied customers are directed to a private text form. Feedback goes directly to management via email/SMS — never published online. Management can follow up and recover the relationship.

4. Review Velocity Compounds

Google's local pack algorithm rewards recency. Restaurants with 5+ new reviews per week rank above restaurants with 500 old reviews and no new activity. Consistent velocity beats raw count.
